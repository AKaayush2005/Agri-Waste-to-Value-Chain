Solutions & Technologies

Innovative technologies for sustainable waste conversion

Biogas Plants

Convert organic waste into clean energy and natural fertilizer through anaerobic digestion.

  • Reduces methane emissions
  • Provides renewable energy
  • Creates nutrient-rich slurry

Biochar Kilns

Pyrolysis technology that converts biomass into carbon-rich biochar for soil enhancement.

  • Improves soil fertility
  • Carbon sequestration
  • Water retention enhancement

Bioplastic Technology

Transform agricultural residues into biodegradable plastic alternatives for packaging.

  • Biodegradable packaging
  • Reduced plastic pollution
  • Food-grade materials
  • Replaces petroleum plastics
  • High value from low-cost residues

Briquetting Machines

Compress agricultural waste into high-density fuel briquettes for clean energy.

  • Clean burning fuel
  • Smoke-free combustion
  • Higher energy density
  • Easy to operate and maintain
  • Reduces open burning

Mushroom Cultivation

Transform crop residues into high-value edible mushrooms and organic fertilizer.

  • High-value food production
  • Short cultivation cycle
  • Rural employment creation
  • Spent substrate as fertilizer

Vermicomposting

Use earthworms to convert organic waste into premium organic fertilizer.

  • Natural soil fertility improvement
  • Low investment requirement
  • Micro-enterprise opportunities
  • Organic farming integration

Technology Selection Guide

Wet, high-moisture residues / manure / food waste

→ Anaerobic digestion or BSF

Low-value dry straw, stalks, husks

→ Torrefaction + pelleting or Pyrolysis → biochar

When you want animal feed value

→ BSF (high protein)

For soil health & carbon credits

→ Biochar (plus microbial optimization)

For fuels/chemicals

→ Biochemical pathway (pretreatment + enzymatic hydrolysis)

For smallholders across many farms

→ Modular hubs + digital aggregation

Implementation & Scale Advice (Practical)

1

Map feedstock streams & seasonality

Build supply curves and cold/wet/dry split. Supply chain modeling drastically reduces risk.

2

Start with pilots that combine two value streams

E.g., BSF larvae → animal feed + frass; or AD for energy + digestate to farmers.

3

Quality & standards

Ensure feed/soil product safety (pathogen control for frass/digestate), and obtain feed/fertiliser approvals where relevant.

4

Finance & aggregation

Consider farmer co-ops, equipment rental/banks, or results-based carbon/ESG finance to de-risk capital costs.

5

Measure & iterate

Track GHG reductions, yields, and revenue per tonne of residue to identify the best scaling pathway.
